Salisbury Steak

Description

This Salisbury steak recipe is a total comfort food classic. It starts with ground beef mixed with breadcrumbs, egg, and a bit of condensed French onion soup to keep things juicy and flavorful. The patties are browned up in a skillet, then simmered in a rich, savory gravy made from the rest of the onion soup, flour, ketchup, Worcestershire sauce, and a hint of mustard. The result is a hearty, satisfying dish that feels like a warm hug on a plate.

Perfect for a cozy night in, this meal pairs beautifully with mashed potatoes to soak up all that delicious gravy. It's easy to make but tastes like you spent hours in the kitchen. Whether you're feeding the fammily or just craving some nostalgic eats, this Salisbury steak hits the spot everytime.

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ pounds of ground beef
  • 1 (10.5 ounce) can of condensed French onion soup
  • ½ cup of dry bread crumbs
  • 1 large egg
  • ¼ teaspoon of salt
  • ⅛ teaspoon of ground black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon of all-purpose flour
  • ¼ cup ketchup
  • ¼ cup water
  • 1 tablepoon of Worcestershire sauce
  • ½ teaspoon mustard powder

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Combine ground beef, 1/3 cup condensed soup, bread crumbs, egg, salt, and black pepper in a large bowl.
  3. Shape into 6 oval patties.
  4. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat; add patties and brown on both sides. Drain excess fat.
  5. Blend remaining soup and flour together in a small bowl until smooth; mix in ketchup, water, Worcestershire sauce, and mustard.
  6. Pour soup mixture over patties in the skillet. Cover, and cook for 20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  7. Serve and enjoy!
