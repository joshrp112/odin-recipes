Ropa Vieja

Description

Ropa Vieja is Cuba's ultimate comfort food, tender shredded flank steak simmered in a rich tomato-based sauce with onions, garlic, and bell peppers. This recipe by Chef John layers in bold spices like cumin, paprika, oregano, and a touch of cayenne, then finishes with briny green olives, capers, and fresh cilantro for that signature cuban flavor. The result? A savory, slighly smoky stew that's deeply satisfying and full of character.

What sets this version apart is the overnight rest-it lets the flavors meld and the beefsoak up every ounce of the sauce. Traditionally served with white rice, black beans, and fried plantains, it's a dish that feels both homey and celebratory. Whether you're new to cuban cuisine or a longtime fan, this Ropa Vieja delivers a taste of Havana in every bite.

Ingredients

  • 1 (1 1/2-pound) flank steak
  • 2 teaspoons of kosher salt
  • 1 teaspoon of freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 pinch of cayenne pepper
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 large red onion, sliced
  • 4 cloves of garlic, sliced
  • 2 teaspoons of ground cumin
  • 2 teaspoons of paprika
  • 1 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • ¼ teaspoon of cayenne pepper
  • ⅛ teaspoon of ground cloves
  • ⅛ teaspoon of ground allspice
  • ½ cup of white wine
  • 1 ½ cups of tomato sauce
  • 1 ½ cups chicken broth
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2 bell peppers, sliced
  • 1 poblano pepper, sliced
  • ½ teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 2 tablespoons of capers, drained
  • 1 cup of pimento-stuffed green olives, sliced
  • 1 teaspoon of white sugar
  • ⅓ cup chopped fresh cilantro
